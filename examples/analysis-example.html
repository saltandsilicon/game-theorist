Game Theory Analysis 22 April 2026
Glossary, game theory
Game
A situation where players make interdependent decisions. Each move depends on what others might do.
Zero-sum
One player's gain is another's loss. The opposite is positive-sum, where both can win.
Nash equilibrium
A combination of strategies where no player gains from changing alone. Stable, not always optimal.
BATNA
Best Alternative To a Negotiated Agreement. Your fallback if the deal fails. Your real position.
Coalition formation
A subset of players who agree to coordinate moves. Can shift the equilibrium against the rest.
Commitment device
An action that removes your own flexibility, making a threat or promise credible.
Common knowledge
A fact that everyone knows, everyone knows that everyone knows, and so on. Critical for coordination.
Repeated game
Players interact more than once. Cooperation becomes possible. Reputation becomes an asset.

Six phases: Deconstruction, Incentive mapping, Strategy space, Equilibrium, Recommendation, Dynamic adaptation.

Part I, Case Study

The OpenAI board crisis

Sequential·4 players·72 hours·17 to 21 November 2023

⚐Players
I.
The non-profit board

Toner, Sutskever, D'Angelo, McCauley.

Stated
Preserve the OpenAI mission and safety mandate.
Actual
Reassert control over the direction of the leading AI lab.
Constraints
No equity, no employee mandate, opaque communication norms.
BATNA
Walk away as the directors who blocked the AI race. Career-ending in tech.
Time horizon
Long, mission-oriented. Underweighted the next 72 hours.
II.
Sam Altman

CEO of OpenAI, central node of the network.

Stated
Continue leading OpenAI through the GPT-4 era.
Actual
Lead whichever entity becomes the leading AI lab. The label matters less than the position.
Constraints
Personal reputation, NDAs, commitments to investors.
BATNA
Microsoft AI lab with cash, compute, and any employee who follows. Strong.
Time horizon
Compressed to hours by the public nature of the firing.
III.

$13B already deployed, exclusive cloud and IP rights.

Stated
Supportive of any outcome that protects the partnership.
Actual
Secure access to the talent and IP regardless of which legal shell holds them.
Constraints
Antitrust scrutiny, board obligations, public market reaction.
BATNA
Absorb the talent into a Microsoft AI lab. Slower, but safe.
Time horizon
Quarters. The race outlasts any single weekend.
IV.
The 770 employees

A pending tender offer, deep loyalty to Sam, mission identity.

Stated

Actual
Protect the upcoming tender offer that would make many of them millionaires.
Constraints
Visa status for many, single-employer income, OpenAI brand on resumes.
BATNA

Time horizon

◈Deconstruction

The board believed it was playing a one-shot principal-agent game. Hire the CEO, fire the CEO, replace the CEO. Standard board prerogative under the OpenAI charter. The legal authority was uncontested.

What was actually being played was a coalition-formation game with three implicit veto players the board had not budgeted for: Microsoft, the employees, and the tender offer that gave the employees a near-term financial reason to act collectively.

The information asymmetry ran in the opposite direction from what the board assumed. The board held private information about Sam (the alleged candor failures). Microsoft and the employees held private information about the cost of his removal: Nadella had a Saturday morning offer ready, the employees had a Slack channel and a draft letter. None of that was visible from the boardroom on Friday afternoon.

The game was sequential. Friday: the board moves. Saturday: Microsoft moves. Sunday: the employees move. Monday: the board faces a coalition it did not see forming. By Tuesday, the equilibrium has flipped and the board is replaced.

⤳Incentive mapping, on the board's side

The board's payoff function valued mission preservation over personal continuity. Three of the four members had no equity, no career risk in the conventional sense, and a strong belief that the safety mandate trumped commercial momentum. They were, in their own model, doing the right thing.

Their failure was not in values. It was in the model of the game. They did not assign a payoff to the response of Microsoft or the employees, because they did not see those parties as players. In a principal-agent game, the agent has no allies. In the actual game, the agent had two coalitions waiting in the wings.

⊞Strategy space

By Sunday evening, the board's options had collapsed. Apply iterated dominance to what remained.

Move Outcome under best response from Sam, Microsoft, employees
Hold Emmett Shear as interim Mass walkout Monday morning. Microsoft hires the entire technical staff. OpenAI is a brand on a building.
Negotiate Sam's return without restructuring Same equilibrium recurs in six months. No credibility gained. The board is replaced anyway.
Sell the company to Microsoft outright Antitrust risk, charter violation, employees still leave. Worst of all paths.
Reinstate Sam, restructure the board, retain dignity Loss of personal seats, but the institution survives. The only path with a positive payoff for the institution.
⚖EquilibriumNash, post-coalition

Sam returns·the board is replaced·Microsoft observes

Stable because the coalition (Sam, Microsoft, employees) had a credible exit. The board's threat lost credibility the moment Nadella sent his Saturday email.
➤Recommendation, retrospective for the board

What follows is the move the board should have played, if their goal was mission preservation rather than personal authority. The framework is symmetric: it works for any board considering the removal of a charismatic founder.

Primary move Negotiate the exit privately first. A public firing creates a public counter-coalition. The board's authority was credible only as long as the move stayed inside the boardroom.
Commitment device Pre-secure a successor with employee credibility. Mira Murati was loyal to Sam. Naming her would have signalled continuity. Naming Emmett Shear signalled rupture.
Signal Frame the action as governance reform, with a published timeline. A clear story removes Microsoft's incentive to intervene as a saviour.
Coalition pre-emption Brief the top fifteen technical leads before announcing. Their public support, or even their public neutrality, breaks the employee coalition before it forms.
Contingency If Microsoft objects, have a board statement ready that frames the partnership as unaffected. Ambiguity is the friend of the firing party. Silence is fatal.
Timing Not on a Friday afternoon before the US Thanksgiving week. The empty calendar gives the counter-coalition seventy-two free hours. Move on a Wednesday morning, with a planned all-hands within four hours.
Coalition size 770 of 800
Time to flip 96 hours
Board members lost 3 of 4
Verdict Mission unchanged
↻Dynamic adaptation

Two years on, the secondary effects are visible.

Other AI labs studied the game and built different governance. Anthropic kept its Long-Term Benefit Trust but paired it with founder-supportive bylaws. xAI never had a non-profit veto. Mistral incorporated as a normal European company. The board veto became a known liability.

Microsoft demonstrated that its leverage extended past the contract. The lesson registered with every other lab dependent on hyperscaler compute. The next AI labs raised more, faster, partly to escape the same dependency.

The board members, in the year after, became cautionary references rather than authorities. The reputation cost turned out to be the dominant cost of the entire episode, larger than any short-term governance victory could have justified.

❧Counter-scenarios
If Microsoft had stayed silent The board would have held Sunday and Monday. Sam would have negotiated from a weaker position. The new equilibrium would have been a partial reinstatement on board-set conditions. The non-profit charter would have survived intact for at least another year.
If Ilya had not flipped Sutskever's public reversal on Sunday signalled the coalition was breaking. With his vote firm, the board would have held a 4 to 0 majority through Monday. The standoff might have extended to a full week, with Microsoft accelerating its talent-absorption plan.
If the tender offer had not been pending The employee coalition would have formed slower and smaller. Many of the 770 signatories were one share-class away from generational wealth. Without that pressure, the letter probably gathers 200 signatures, not 770. The board survives.
If Helen Toner had a different communication style The board's stated reason ("not consistently candid") gave no path to settlement. A more flexible framing would have allowed Sam a face-saving exit and the board a face-saving reform. The rigidity of the message foreclosed the bargaining space.
ΦLesson

A board's authority is credible only inside the building. The moment the move becomes public, the game expands to every player with a stake in the outcome, and the new equilibrium answers to the largest coalition, not the original chart.
